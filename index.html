"This proposal outlines a compensation review based on national averages for comparable hybrid remote roles that combine customer service, marketing analytics, CRM management, and operational oversight."

1. Executive Summary

In my current position at PRP MGMT, I serve as the sole remote agent handling inbound calls for 15 self-storage facilities while simultaneously managing several operational and marketing functions. My work includes organizing social-media leads into two CRMs, conducting lead follow-ups, managing tenant complaint tickets, attending and contributing to marketing meetings, logging content-performance data, and auditing sales calls for corporate review.

I perform all of these functions as a 1099 contractor with no employer-paid benefits, PTO, or tax contributions. On top of that, I regularly interact with Spanish-speaking customers at a conversational level to resolve storage-related requests—providing semi-bilingual customer support that improves service access and retention.

Because this hybrid role combines customer service, CRM management, social-media operations, data analysis, and bilingual communication, I conducted an evidence-based compensation review using current federal and industry data. The analysis uses official numbers from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), ZipRecruiter, Zippia, and the IRS, applying the official BLS Employer Costs for Employee Compensation (ECEC) multiplier to convert W-2 wages to 1099 parity.

According to this research, comparable national roles range between $19 and $37 per hour (W-2). After applying the 1099 conversion factor, the appropriate contractor parity range is $34–$35 per hour.

At my current rate of $25 per hour, I am compensated roughly 36–40 percent below verified national parity. Adjusting my rate to the documented range of $34–$35 per hour would align my pay with national data and the full scope of my contributions to PRP MGMT.
